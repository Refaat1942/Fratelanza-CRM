## Learning objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

1. Classify **antiemetics** by receptor
2. Identify **Motilium, Primperan, Zofran**
3. Counsel **IBS dietary management**
4. Explain **prokinetic cardiac risk** (domperidone)
5. Select **IBS agents** — mebeverine, trimebutine

---

## 1. Antiemetics
| Drug | Brand | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Metoclopramide | Primperan | D2 antagonist — EPS risk |
| Domperidone | Motilium | D2 — less CNS; QT risk |
| Ondansetron | Zofran | 5-HT3 — chemo/post-op |
| Dimenhydrinate | Dramamine | Antihistamine — motion sickness |

## 2. IBS Egypt
Mebeverine (Duspatalin), Trimebutine (Polybalm), Peppermint oil. Diet: low FODMAP counseling.

**Counseling (Arabic):** "موتيليوم قبل الأكل ب15-30 دقيقة. قلل الضغط والقهوة لمتلازمة القولون."

## 3. Scenario
> Motilium requested long-term — counsel max 7 days per EMA guidance, QT monitoring.

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — EG-MED-05-L02**
> - Primperan EPS risk especially young
> - Motilium QT risk — short courses
> - Zofran for severe nausea
> - IBS needs dietary counseling
> - Mebeverine antispasmodic common
> - Avoid metoclopramide long-term
