## Course description

This course provides comprehensive, evidence-based training on cardiovascular medications available in the Egyptian pharmaceutical market. Learners will master mechanisms of action, Egyptian brand identification, dosing, interactions, patient counseling in Arabic, and pharmacy practice scenarios aligned with Egyptian Society of Cardiology guidelines.

## Learning outcomes (course level)

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. Identify **50+ cardiovascular drugs** by generic name and Egyptian trade name
2. Apply **ESC/ESC-Egypt hypertension guidelines** (target BP <140/90 mmHg general; <130/80 high-risk)
3. Counsel patients on **antihypertensive adherence** using evidence-based techniques
4. Recognize **high-risk interactions** (warfarin, NSAIDs, potassium-sparing combinations)
5. Recommend **appropriate generic substitutes** available from Egyptian manufacturers
6. Manage **common pharmacy scenarios** (BP check, medication refill, side effect complaint)

## Egyptian market context

- **Hypertension prevalence in Egypt:** ~26–30% of adults (WHO STEPwise survey data)
- **Major local manufacturers:** Pharco, EIPICO, Memphis, Sedico, Sigma, Amoun
- **Multinational brands:** Novartis, AstraZeneca, Pfizer, Sanofi — often higher price
- **Generic penetration:** >70% of cardiovascular prescriptions in public sector
