## 1. Antiplatelet agents

### 1.1 Mechanism

```
Platelet aggregation pathway:
Arachidonic acid → Thromboxane A2 (aspirin blocks COX-1 irreversibly)
ADP → P2Y12 receptor (clopidogrel, ticagrelor block)
Glycoprotein IIb/IIIa (abciximab — hospital use)
```

**Evidence:** CAPRIE — clopidogrel modestly superior to aspirin in PAD. PLATO — ticagrelor superior to clopidogrel post-ACS.

### 1.2 Antiplatelets — Egyptian market

| Generic | Brand (Egypt) | Manufacturer | Strengths | Indication | Approx. EGP/month |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Aspirin** | Aspocid, Aspirin Protect | CID, Bayer / local | 75, 100, 300mg | CV prophylaxis, ACS | 20–60 |
| **Clopidogrel** | Plavix, Clopidogrel | Sanofi / Amoun, Memphis | 75mg | ACS, PCI, stroke | 150–400 |
| **Ticagrelor** | Brilique | AstraZeneca | 90mg BD | ACS (preferred over clopidogrel) | 800–1500 |
| **Prasugrel** | Efient | Lilly (limited) | 10mg | ACS-PCI | 600–1000 |
| **Dipyridamole + ASA** | Aggrenox | Limited | Combo | Secondary stroke prevention |

### 1.3 Aspirin counseling

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Dose** | 75–100mg OD for CV prevention; 300mg loading in ACS |
| **Contraindications** | Active GI bleed, aspirin asthma, children <16 (Reye syndrome) |
| **Interactions** | ↑ bleeding with NSAIDs, warfarin, SSRIs; ibuprofen may block aspirin cardioprotection if taken before aspirin |

## 2. Anticoagulants

### 2.1 Warfarin (vitamin K antagonist)

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| **Brand (Egypt)** | Marevan (Taro), Warfarin generic (EIPICO) |
| **Mechanism** | Inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase → ↓ Factors II, VII, IX, X |
| **Monitoring** | INR target 2–3 (AF, VTE); 2.5–3.5 mechanical mitral valve |
| **Dosing** | Individualized; typical 3–5mg OD Egyptian maintenance |
| **Approx. cost** | 30–80 EGP/month — very affordable |

**Warfarin interactions (critical in Egypt):**

| ↑ INR (bleeding risk) | ↓ INR (clot risk) |
|---|---|
| Amiodarone (Cordarone — very common) | Rifampicin (TB treatment common) |
| Metronidazole (Flagyl) | Carbamazepine |
| Fluconazole (Diflucan) | Phenytoin |
| Paracetamol high dose | St John's wort |
| Cranberry juice (large amounts) | Vitamin K-rich foods (variable) |

### 2.2 Direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs)

| Generic | Brand (Egypt) | Target | Renal dosing | Approx. EGP/month |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rivaroxaban** | Xarelto | Factor Xa | Avoid if CrCl <15; reduce if CrCl 15–49 | 600–1200 |
| **Apixaban** | Eliquis | Factor Xa | Reduce if ≥2 of: age ≥80, ≤60kg, Cr ≥1.5 | 700–1400 |
| **Dabigatran** | Pradaxa | Thrombin (IIa) | Avoid if CrCl <30 | 500–1000 |
| **Edoxaban** | Lixiana | Factor Xa | Limited availability Egypt | 600–1100 |

**DOAC advantages:** No routine INR monitoring; fixed dosing; rapid onset.
**DOAC cautions:** No widely available reversal (andexanet for apixaban/rivaroxaban — limited); renal dose adjustment essential.

**Counseling script — Warfarin (Arabic):**

> "ماريفان لازم يتاخد نفس الساعة كل يوم. لازم تحلل INR بانتظام حسب ما الدكتور قال. خضروات ورقية زي الجرجير والسبانخ فيها فيتامين ك — متغيرش كميتها فجأة، خليك ثابت. لو لاحظت نزيف لثة، بول برتقالي، أو كدمات من غير سبب، روح للدكتور فوراً. متاخدش أي دواء جديد من الصيدلية من غير ما تقول إنك على ماريفان."

## 3. Indications summary

| Condition | Standard therapy (Egypt) |
|---|---|
| AF (non-valvular) | DOAC preferred over warfarin (ESC) — if affordable; warfarin if cost-limited |
| Mechanical heart valve | Warfarin ONLY — DOACs contraindicated |
| DVT/PE | DOAC or warfarin 3–6+ months |
| Post-PCI (ACS) | DAPT: aspirin + clopidogrel/ticagrelor 12 months |
| Stroke prevention | Clopidogrel or aspirin per guideline |

## 4. Pharmacy practice scenario

### Scenario 1.6 — Warfarin + antibiotic
> *Patient on Marevan 5mg daily (INR usually 2.5). Prescribed Flagyl (metronidazole) for dental infection. INR today 4.8.*

**Response:**
1. Recognize metronidazole-warfarin interaction — potent CYP inhibition
2. Urgent physician contact — likely hold/skip warfarin dose
3. Counsel bleeding signs; avoid NSAIDs
4. Document interaction for future reference
5. Explain INR recheck in 2–3 days after antibiotic course

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — Lesson 1.6**
> - Aspocid (aspirin 75–100mg) — cornerstone of CV prevention; very cheap in Egypt
> - Plavix (clopidogrel) — Amoun/Memphis generics significantly reduce cost
> - Marevan (warfarin) — still widely used; requires INR monitoring; many interactions
> - Xarelto, Eliquis available but costly — insurance/private sector mainly
> - Mechanical valves: warfarin mandatory — never substitute DOAC
> - Flagyl, Diflucan, Cordarone — dangerous warfarin interactions in Egyptian practice
