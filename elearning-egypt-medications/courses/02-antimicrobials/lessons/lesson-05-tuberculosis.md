## 1. First-line anti-TB drugs (RIPE)

| Drug | Mechanism | Egyptian brands | Key toxicity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rifampicin** | Inhibits RNA polymerase | Rifadin, Rimactane, EIPICO generic | Hepatotoxicity, orange fluids, CYP450 inducer |
| **Isoniazid** | Inhibits mycolic acid synthesis | Hyzide, Isoniazid (Memphis) | Hepatotoxicity, peripheral neuropathy |
| **Pyrazinamide** | Unknown — acidic environment | Pyrazinamide generic | Hepatotoxicity, hyperuricemia |
| **Ethambutol** | Inhibits arabinosyl transferase | Myambutol, generic | Optic neuritis (color vision) |

### 1.1 Fixed-dose combinations (Egypt)

| FDC | Contents | Phase |
|---|---|---|
| **4FDC** | RHZE × 4 drugs | Intensive 2 months |
| **2FDC** | RH continuation | Continuation 4 months |
| **Rifampicin + isoniazid** | RH | Continuation phase |

**Standard regimen:** 2HRZE/4HR (6 months total drug-sensitive TB).

## 2. Egypt National TB Program

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Incidence** | ~11–15 per 100,000 (WHO estimate; higher in urban slums) |
| **MDR-TB** | ~3% new cases; higher in retreatment |
| **DOTS** | Directly Observed Treatment Short-course — supervised dosing |
| **Pharmacy role** | Refer suspected cases; support adherence counseling; NO unsupervised TB drug dispensing without NTP protocol |
| **BCG** | Universal neonatal vaccination Egypt |

**Rifampicin interactions (critical in Egypt):**

| Drug affected | Effect |
|---|---|
| Warfarin (Marevan) | ↓ INR dramatically — adjust dose |
| Oral contraceptives | ↓ efficacy — alternative contraception |
| Antiretrovirals | Complex — specialist management |
| Oral hypoglycemics | ↓ levels |

**INH + pyridoxine:** Pyridoxine 25mg OD prevents peripheral neuropathy — co-prescribe in Egypt.

**Counseling script (Arabic):**

> "أدوية السل لازم تتاخد كل يوم في نفس الوقت لمدة 6 شهور كاملة — حتى لو حسيت بتحسن من أول شهر. البول والعرق ممكن يلونوا برتقالي — ده طبيعي من الريفامبيسين. لو حسيت اصفرار في العين أو تعب شديد، روح للدكتور فوراً. متوقفش الدوا من نفسك أبداً."

## 4. Pharmacy scenario

### Scenario 2.5 — TB patient on OCP
> *Female TB patient on RHZE regimen starting month 1. Takes Microgynon (combined OCP). Asks if safe.*

**Response:**
1. Rifampicin induces CYP enzymes → ↓ ethinylestradiol levels → contraceptive failure
2. Urgent physician referral for alternative: depot injection, IUD, or double barrier
3. Counsel TB adherence — 6 months minimum
4. Document interaction counseled
5. Do not recommend "doubling" OCP dose without specialist

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — EG-MED-02-L05**
> - RIPE regimen: 2 months intensive + 4 months continuation
> - Rifampicin — orange body fluids normal; massive CYP inducer
> - INH requires pyridoxine co-prescription for neuropathy prevention
> - DOTS — directly observed therapy via Egypt NTP
> - Rifampicin renders oral contraceptives unreliable
> - MDR-TB ~3% — specialist centers only for second-line drugs
