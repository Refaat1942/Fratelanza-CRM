## 1. Aminoglycosides

| Drug | Brand (Egypt) | Route | Monitoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gentamicin** | Gentamycin | IV/IM | Trough levels, creatinine |
| **Amikacin** | Amikin | IV/IM | Same — broader spectrum |
| **Tobramycin** | Tobramycin | IV/inhaled | CF, Pseudomonas |

**Mechanism:** Irreversible 30S ribosomal binding → bactericidal. Concentration-dependent killing.

**Toxicity:** Nephrotoxicity (usually reversible), ototoxicity (often irreversible). Once-daily dosing reduces toxicity.

**Contraindications:** Myasthenia gravis (relative). Avoid with furosemide, vancomycin, other nephrotoxins.

## 2. Metronidazole — Egyptian market

| Brand | Manufacturer | Strengths | Approx. EGP |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flagyl** | Sanofi / EIPICO generic | 250, 500mg; susp | 30–80/course |
| **Metronidazole** | Pharco, Memphis | Generic tabs | 20–60/course |

| Indication | Dosing |
|---|---|
| Anaerobic infections | 400–500mg TDS × 7–10 days |
| C. difficile | 400mg TDS × 10–14 days |
| Giardia/amebiasis | 400mg TDS × 5–7 days |
| Trichomonas | 2g single dose or 400mg BD × 7 days |
| Bacterial vaginosis | 400mg BD × 7 days |

**Critical interaction:** Alcohol → disulfiram-like reaction (nausea, vomiting, flushing). Avoid during treatment AND 48 hours after.

**Counseling script (Arabic):**

> "فلاجيل ممنوع مع الكحول خالص — ولا حتى جرعة صغيرة — وكمان لمدة يومين بعد آخر قرص. لو حسيت تنميل في الرجلين أو اليدين، كلم الدكتور."

## 3. Antiparasitics — Egypt relevance

| Drug | Brand | Indication | Dosing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Albendazole** | Zentel, Andazol | Ascariasis, hookworm, neurocysticercosis | 400mg single or BD × 3 days |
| **Mebendazole** | Vermox | Pinworm, roundworm | 100mg BD × 3 days |
| **Praziquantel** | Biltricide | **Schistosomiasis** (endemic Nile Delta) | 40mg/kg × 1–3 doses |
| **Tinidazole** | Fasigyn | Giardia, amoebiasis | 2g single dose |

**Schistosomiasis:** Major public health issue in rural Egypt — praziquantel mass treatment campaigns.

---

## 4. Antinal & nifuroxazide

| Product | Active | Egyptian practice issue |
|---|---|---|
| **Antinal** | Nifuroxazide | Sold OTC for pediatric diarrhea — NOT substitute for ORS |
| **Normix** | Rifaximin | Traveler's diarrhea, hepatic encephalopathy |

**Correct pediatric diarrhea management:** ORS + zinc ± probiotics; antibiotics only for dysentery (bloody stool) or cholera per guidelines.

---

## 5. Pharmacy scenario

### Scenario 2.4 — Flagyl and alcohol
> *Patient on Flagyl 500mg TDS for dental infection asks if wine at wedding is safe "just one glass."*

**Response:**
1. Strict prohibition — disulfiram reaction risk
2. Explain symptoms: severe nausea, vomiting, flushing, tachycardia
3. Abstain during course + 48h after last dose
4. Complete full antibiotic course
5. Report peripheral neuropathy if tingling develops

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — EG-MED-02-L04**
> - Gentamicin — hospital use; TDM mandatory; ototoxicity irreversible
> - Flagyl — most recognized metronidazole brand; EIPICO generic available
> - NO alcohol with metronidazole during + 48h after
> - Biltricide (praziquantel) — schistosomiasis endemic in Egypt
> - Antinal OTC misuse — ORS is first-line for pediatric diarrhea
> - Zentel (albendazole) — common helminth therapy in Egypt
