## Learning objectives

1. Describe **nociceptive vs neuropathic** pain
2. Apply **WHO analgesic ladder**
3. Identify **red flag pain**
4. Counsel on **pain expectations** in Arabic

---

## 1. Pain types
Nociceptive: tissue injury. Neuropathic: nerve damage — burning, shooting.

## 2. WHO ladder
| Step | Drugs (Egypt) |
|---|---|
| 1 Mild | Cetal, Brufen |
| 2 Moderate | + Tramal (tramadol) |
| 3 Severe | Morphine (hospital) |
| Adjuvant | Amitriptyline, Lyrica |

**Counseling (Arabic):** "الألم العصبي محتاج أدوية مختلفة. متزودش الجرعة من نفسك — اتبع الدكتور."

## 3. Scenario
> Thunderclap headache — refer emergency immediately.

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — EG-MED-04-L01**
> - WHO ladder guides stepwise analgesia
> - Neuropathic pain needs adjuvants
> - Red flags need urgent referral
> - Tramadol step 2 — controlled Egypt
> - OTC paracetamol/NSAIDs step 1

# Lesson 4.2: Paracetamol & NSAIDs — Cetal, Brufen, Voltaren

|---|---|

---

## Learning objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

1. Compare **paracetamol vs NSAID** mechanisms
2. Identify **Cetal, Brufen, Voltaren** brands
3. Counsel **GI, renal, CV risks**
4. Apply **4g/day paracetamol limit**
5. Manage **NSAID + antihypertensive** interactions

---

## 1. Paracetamol — Egyptian market
| Brand | Manufacturer | Strength | Max dose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cetal | EIPICO | 500mg | 4g/day |
| Panadol | GSK | 500mg | 4g/day |
| Paramol | Pharco | 500mg | 4g/day |

Hepatotoxicity in overdose. Safe in pregnancy (therapeutic doses).

## 2. NSAIDs
| Generic | Brand | Form | Key risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ibuprofen | Brufen | Tabs, gel | GI, renal, ↑BP |
| Diclofenac | Voltaren, Cataflam | Tabs, gel | CV risk oral |
| Naproxen | Naproxen | Tabs | Longer acting |
| Ketoprofen | Ketofan | Gel | Topical safer |

**Contraindications:** Active ulcer, severe CKD, 3rd trimester, aspirin asthma.

**Counseling (Arabic):** "الباراسيتامول متعديش 8 أقراص في اليوم. البروفين والفولتارين مع الأكل. لو عندك ضغط أو كلى، اسأل الدكتور."

## 3. Scenario
> Patient on Tareg requests Voltaren for back pain. Counsel NSAID worsens BP/renal function; suggest paracetamol, topical gel, physician review.

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — EG-MED-04-L02**
> - Cetal max 4g/day — hepatotoxicity overdose
> - Brufen/Voltaren with food
> - NSAIDs raise BP — Egyptian HTN interaction
> - Topical diclofenac lower systemic risk
> - Avoid NSAIDs 3rd trimester
> - Diclofenac higher CV risk than naproxen

4. Identify **Tramal, Contramal**
5. Address **tramadol abuse epidemic**

---

## 1. Opioids Egypt
| Drug | Brand | Control |
|---|---|---|
| Tramadol | Tramal, Contramal | Controlled Rx |
| Codeine | Codeine compound | Controlled |
| Morphine | MST | Strict controlled |

## 2. Tramadol abuse epidemic
EDA tightened controls after widespread misuse. Pharmacist must verify Rx, limit quantities, refuse doctor shopping.

**Side effects:** Nausea, constipation, seizures (OD), serotonin syndrome with SSRIs.

**Counseling (Arabic):** "ترامادول محتاج روشتة ومتابعة. ميسببش إدمان لو اتاخد صح. اشرب مية وكل ألياف للإمساك."

## 3. Scenario
> Tramal request without Rx — refuse, counsel physician visit, watch for misuse patterns.

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — EG-MED-04-L03**
> - Tramadol controlled — Rx mandatory
> - Tramal main brand Egypt
> - Abuse epidemic shaped regulations
> - Constipation counseling essential
> - Tramadol + SSRI serotonin syndrome
> - Never dispense without valid Rx
