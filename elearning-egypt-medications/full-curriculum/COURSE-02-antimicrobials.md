## Learning objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

1. Define **antimicrobial stewardship (AMS)** and its role in Egyptian pharmacy
2. Apply **WHO AWaRe classification** (Access, Watch, Reserve) to Egyptian formulary
3. Explain **EDA prescription regulations** and pharmacist dispensing obligations
4. Identify **Egyptian resistance patterns** for key pathogens (E. coli, Klebsiella, MRSA)
5. Counsel patients on **appropriate antibiotic use** and completion of course in Arabic

---

## 1. Antimicrobial stewardship framework

### 1.1 Core principles

```
Stewardship goals:
├── Improve patient outcomes
├── Reduce antimicrobial resistance (AMR)
├── Minimize adverse effects (C. difficile, nephrotoxicity)
└── Control healthcare costs
```

**WHO AWaRe classification:**

| Category | Meaning | Egyptian examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Access** | First/second choice — widely available | Amoxicillin, amoxicillin-clavulanate, cephalexin, trimethoprim |
| **Watch** | Higher resistance risk — restrict use | Ciprofloxacin, azithromycin, ceftriaxone, clindamycin |
| **Reserve** | Last-line — specialist use | Meropenem, colistin, linezolid |

**Evidence:** WHO Global Action Plan on AMR (2015); Egyptian National AMR Action Plan aligned with WHO.

### 1.2 Egyptian resistance data (summary)

| Pathogen | Resistance concern (Egypt surveillance) |
|---|---|
| E. coli (UTI) | ESBL production 30–50% in hospital isolates; high fluoroquinolone resistance |
| Klebsiella | Carbapenem resistance emerging in ICU settings |
| S. aureus | MRSA 20–40% in some hospital surveys |
| S. pneumoniae | Penicillin/macrolide resistance variable |
| M. tuberculosis | MDR-TB ~3% new cases (WHO Egypt estimate) |

### 1.3 EDA regulatory framework

| Regulation | Pharmacy implication |
|---|---|
| Prescription-only antimicrobials | Must not dispense without valid Rx |
| Retention of Rx copies | 2 years minimum per Egyptian pharmacy law |
| Controlled antimicrobials (TB, some IV) | Hospital/specialist channels |
| OTC misuse concern | Nifuroxazide, some antidiarrheals sold OTC — stewardship issue |

**Counseling script (Arabic):**

> "المضاد الحيوي بيعالج البكتيريا بس، مش الفيروسات زي البرد والإنفلونزا. لازم تكمل الكورس كله حتى لو حسيت بتحسن. متشاركش المضاد مع حد تاني ومتستخدمش مضاد قديم من عندك. ده بيخلي البكتيريا تقاوم الدوا."

---

## 2. Pharmacist stewardship interventions

| Intervention | Example |
|---|---|
| Validate indication | Question fluoroquinolone for uncomplicated UTI when nitrofurantoin/cephalexin appropriate |
| Duration check | Amoxicillin 5–7 days for otitis; not 14 days routinely |
| DDI screening | Macrolide + statin → myopathy; metronidazole + warfarin |
| Patient education | Arabic counseling on adherence and resistance |
| Referral | Persistent fever >48h on antibiotics → physician |

---

## 3. Pharmacy practice scenario

### Scenario 2.1 — Viral URI antibiotic request
> *Mother requests amoxicillin for 4-year-old with runny nose and cough 2 days, no fever. Says neighbor's child got same antibiotic.*

**Response:**
1. Explain viral etiology — antibiotics not indicated
2. Recommend supportive care: saline nasal drops, paracetamol if fever, fluids
3. Red flags for referral: fever >39°C, difficulty breathing, >10 days symptoms
4. Do NOT dispense antibiotic without prescription regardless
5. Document counseling provided

---

## Key takeaways

> 📌 **Key Takeaways — EG-MED-02-L01**
> - AWaRe: Access antibiotics first line; Watch/Reserve need justification
> - Egyptian E. coli UTI: high FQ resistance — avoid empiric ciprofloxacin when possible
> - Pharmacists must enforce prescription-only rules — no antibiotic OTC dispensing
> - Nifuroxazide OTC misuse contributes to resistance concerns in Egypt
> - Complete course counseling prevents partial treatment resistance
> - Egyptian National AMR Plan — pharmacy is frontline stewardship

## 1. Mechanism & resistance

```
Beta-lactam ring → binds PBPs → inhibits cell wall synthesis → bactericidal
Beta-lactamase enzymes → hydrolyze ring → resistance
Clavulanate/sulbactam → beta-lactamase inhibitors → restore activity
```

**Evidence:** Amoxicillin-clavulanate remains first-line for many community infections per Egyptian MOH essential medicines list.

---

| **Amoxicillin-clavulanate** | Augmentin, Clavimox | GSK / Amoun, Memphis | 625mg, 1g tabs; susp | Sinusitis, bite wounds, resistant otitis | 80–250/course |

### 2.1 Dosing & counseling

| Drug | Adult dosing | Key counseling |
|---|---|---|
| Amoxicillin | 500mg TDS or 1g BD | Take with or without food; complete 5–7 days |
| Augmentin 625mg | 1 tab TDS | Take at start of meals — reduces GI upset |
| Augmentin susp | Weight-based pediatrics | Refrigerate; shake well; measure with syringe |

**Contraindications:** True penicillin anaphylaxis. **Interactions:** Probenecid ↑ levels; allopurinol + amoxicillin → rash.

## 4. Pharmacy practice scenario

### Scenario 2.2 — Augmentin diarrhea
> *Patient on Augmentin 625mg TDS day 4 for sinusitis. Watery diarrhea 3×/day, no blood, mild cramping.*

**Response:**
1. Recognize common antibiotic-associated diarrhea (up to 25% with amox-clav)
2. Counsel hydration, probiotics may help; take with meals
3. Red flags for C. difficile: blood, fever, severe pain — urgent referral
4. Do not automatically stop — contact prescriber if moderate-severe
5. No loperamide without physician advice in infectious diarrhea

## 1. Macrolides

### 1.1 Mechanism & evidence

Bind 50S ribosomal subunit → inhibit protein synthesis. Azithromycin achieves high tissue concentrations with short courses.

| Generic | Brand (Egypt) | Manufacturer | Dosing | Approx. EGP |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Azithromycin** | Zithromax, Azithrocin | Pfizer / Pharco, Memphis | 500mg OD × 3 days | 80–200 |
| **Clarithromycin** | Klacid, Clabact | Abbott / generic | 500mg BD × 7 days | 150–350 |
| **Erythromycin** | Erythrocin | Limited | 250–500mg QDS | 40–100 |

**Indications:** Atypical pneumonia, strep pharyngitis (pen allergy), H. pylori triple therapy (with PPI + amoxicillin).

**Interactions:** ↑ statin myopathy risk; ↑ warfarin INR; QT prolongation with amiodarone (Cordarone).

---

## 2. Fluoroquinolones (AWaRe WATCH)

| Generic | Brand (Egypt) | Dosing | Key risks |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ciprofloxacin** | Ciprobay, Ciprodar | 500mg BD | Tendon rupture, QT, CNS effects |
| **Levofloxacin** | Tavanic, Levoxin | 500mg OD | Respiratory infections |
| **Moxifloxacin** | Avalox | 400mg OD | QT prolongation |

**Egyptian resistance:** E. coli fluoroquinolone resistance 50–70% in many hospital surveys — avoid empiric cipro for uncomplicated cystitis.

| Contraindication | Interaction |
|---|---|
| Children <18 (cartilage damage) | Antacids ↓ absorption — separate 2h |
| Pregnancy | Corticosteroids ↑ tendon rupture risk |
| History FQ tendon disorder | Warfarin ↑ INR |

## 1. Aminoglycosides

| Drug | Brand (Egypt) | Route | Monitoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gentamicin** | Gentamycin | IV/IM | Trough levels, creatinine |
| **Amikacin** | Amikin | IV/IM | Same — broader spectrum |
| **Tobramycin** | Tobramycin | IV/inhaled | CF, Pseudomonas |

**Mechanism:** Irreversible 30S ribosomal binding → bactericidal. Concentration-dependent killing.

**Toxicity:** Nephrotoxicity (usually reversible), ototoxicity (often irreversible). Once-daily dosing reduces toxicity.

**Contraindications:** Myasthenia gravis (relative). Avoid with furosemide, vancomycin, other nephrotoxins.

---

## 2. Metronidazole — Egyptian market

| Brand | Manufacturer | Strengths | Approx. EGP |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flagyl** | Sanofi / EIPICO generic | 250, 500mg; susp | 30–80/course |
| **Metronidazole** | Pharco, Memphis | Generic tabs | 20–60/course |

| Indication | Dosing |
|---|---|
| Anaerobic infections | 400–500mg TDS × 7–10 days |
| C. difficile | 400mg TDS × 10–14 days |
| Giardia/amebiasis | 400mg TDS × 5–7 days |
| Trichomonas | 2g single dose or 400mg BD × 7 days |
| Bacterial vaginosis | 400mg BD × 7 days |

**Critical interaction:** Alcohol → disulfiram-like reaction (nausea, vomiting, flushing). Avoid during treatment AND 48 hours after.

**Counseling script (Arabic):**

> "فلاجيل ممنوع مع الكحول خالص — ولا حتى جرعة صغيرة — وكمان لمدة يومين بعد آخر قرص. لو حسيت تنميل في الرجلين أو اليدين، كلم الدكتور."

| Element | Details |
|---|---|

|---|---|
