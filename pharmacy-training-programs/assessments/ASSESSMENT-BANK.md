## Sample calculations — Module 8

**C1.** Child 10 kg, dose 20 mg/kg/day amoxicillin, divided BD. Per dose in mg?
> Answer: 10 × 20 = 200 mg/day ÷ 2 = **100 mg per dose**

**C2.** Adult eGFR 22 mL/min. On gabapentin 300mg TDS. Standard dose 900mg/day. Recommended adjustment?
> Answer: eGFR 15–29 → reduce by 50% → **450 mg/day** (150 mg TDS)

**C3.** Digoxin loading dose: Vd=7L/kg, target=1mcg/L, F=0.7, patient 60kg.
> Answer: (7×60×1)/0.7 = **600 mcg = 0.6 mg**

## Case study rubric (generic)

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Adequate (2) | Fail (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Problem identification | All DRPs identified | Most identified | Some missed | Major misses |
| Scientific rationale | Evidence-based reasoning | Mostly correct | Partial | Incorrect |
| Action plan | Complete, prioritized | Appropriate | Incomplete | Dangerous |
| Communication | Clear SBAR to prescriber | Adequate | Vague | No action |
| Documentation | Full SOAP note | Partial | Minimal | None |

**Pass:** Average ≥2.5 per criterion

## Final comprehensive exam (optional — 50 questions)

Administered after all 10 modules. Covers:
- 20 questions pharmacology/PK (Module 2)
- 15 questions clinical checking/interactions (Modules 3, 5)
- 10 questions special populations (Module 8)
- 5 questions law/ethics (Module 10)

**Pass:** ≥70% (35/50) for program certificate.

## Trainer notes

- Randomize question order per trainee
- Case studies should use anonymized real prescriptions where possible
- OSCE stations require 2 trained assessors for reliability
- Allow BNF/drug reference during calculation exams (not MCQ)
- Re-assessment allowed after 2 weeks additional study
