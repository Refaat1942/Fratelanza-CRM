## Primary references by module

### Module 1 — Foundations
1. World Health Organization. (2017). *Medication Without Harm: WHO Global Patient Safety Challenge.* Geneva: WHO. https://www.who.int/teams/integrated-health-services/patient-safety/research/medication-safety
2. Hepler CD, Strand LM. (1990). Opportunities and responsibilities in pharmaceutical care. *American Journal of Hospital Pharmacy*, 47(3), 533–543.
3. Chisholm-Burns MA, et al. (2010). US pharmacists' effect as team members on patient care. *Medical Care*, 48(10), 923–933.
4. Reason J. (1990). *Human Error.* Cambridge University Press.
5. Beauchamp TL, Childress JF. (2019). *Principles of Biomedical Ethics* (8th ed.). Oxford University Press.

### Module 2 — Pharmacology
6. Brunton LL, Hilal-Dandan R, Knollmann BC. (2023). *Goodman & Gilman's The Pharmacological Basis of Therapeutics* (14th ed.). McGraw-Hill.
7. Rowland M, Tozer TN. (2011). *Clinical Pharmacokinetics and Pharmacodynamics* (4th ed.). Lippincott Williams & Wilkins.
8. FDA. (2020). *Bioequivalence Studies with Pharmacokinetic Endpoints for Drugs Submitted Under ANDA.* Silver Spring: FDA.
9. Desta Z, et al. (2002). Clinical significance of the cytochrome P450 2D6 genetic polymorphism. *Clinical Pharmacokinetics*, 41(12), 913–935.

### Module 3 — Prescription Science
10. NICE. (2015). NG5: Medicines optimisation. London: NICE.
11. Bates DW, et al. (1995). Incidence of adverse drug events and potential adverse drug events. *JAMA*, 274(1), 29–34.
12. Institute for Safe Medication Practices. *List of Error-Prone Abbreviations, Symbols, and Dose Designations.* https://www.ismp.org
13. American Academy of Pediatrics. (2024). *Red Book: Report of the Committee on Infectious Diseases* (current ed.).

### Module 4 — Dispensing Safety
14. Institute for Safe Medication Practices. *ISMP List of High-Alert Medications in Community/Ambulatory Healthcare.* (2020).
15. James KL, et al. (2009). Incidence, type and causes of dispensing errors. *Research in Social and Administrative Pharmacy*, 5(4), 323–336.
16. Poon EG, et al. (2006). Effect of bar-code technology on the safety of medication administration. *BMJ*, 332(7537), 234–237.

### Module 5 — Interactions
17. Baxter K (ed). (2023). *Stockley's Drug Interactions* (12th ed.). London: Pharmaceutical Press.
18. Naranjo CA, et al. (1981). A method for estimating the probability of adverse drug reactions. *Clinical Pharmacology & Therapeutics*, 30(2), 239–245.
19. FDA. (2016). FDA Drug Safety Communication: FDA warns about serious risks of combining opioid pain medicines with benzodiazepines.

### Module 6 — Counseling
20. World Health Organization. (2003). *Adherence to Long-Term Therapies: Evidence for Action.* Geneva: WHO.
21. Kripalani S, et al. (2007). Deficits in communication and information transfer between hospital-based and primary care physicians. *Archives of Internal Medicine*, 167(13), 1380–1384.
22. Global Initiative for Asthma (GINA). (2024). *Global Strategy for Asthma Management and Prevention.* https://ginasthma.org
23. American Diabetes Association. (2024). Standards of Care in Diabetes — 2024. *Diabetes Care*, 47(Suppl 1).

### Module 7 — Stability
24. ICH. (2003). Q1A(R2): Stability Testing of New Drug Substances and Products.
25. USP Convention. USP <659> Packaging and Storage Requirements. USP <1079> Good Storage and Shipping Practices.
26. Lyon RC, et al. (2006). Stability profiles of drug products extended beyond labeled expiration dates. *Journal of Pharmaceutical Sciences*, 95(7), 1549–1560.

### Module 8 — Special Populations
27. American Geriatrics Society Beers Criteria® Update Expert Panel. (2023). American Geriatrics Society 2023 updated AGS Beers Criteria®. *Journal of the American Geriatrics Society*, 71(7), 2052–2081.
28. Taketomo CK, et al. *Pediatric & Neonatal Dosage Handbook* (current ed.). Hudson, OH: Lexicomp.
29. National Library of Medicine. *Drugs and Lactation Database (LactMed).* https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK501922/

### Module 9 — Infection Control
30. World Health Organization. (2009). *WHO Guidelines on Hand Hygiene in Health Care: First Global Patient Safety Challenge Clean Care is Safer Care.* Geneva: WHO.
31. USP Convention. USP <797> Pharmaceutical Compounding — Sterile Preparations.

### Module 10 — Law & Ethics (Egypt)
