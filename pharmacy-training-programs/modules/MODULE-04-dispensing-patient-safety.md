## Section 4.1 — Medication error science

### 4.1.1 WHO Global Patient Safety Challenge data

| Statistic | Source | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| 1 in 20 patients harmed by healthcare | WHO, 2019 | Systemic problem requiring culture change |
| Medication errors: ~$42 billion/year globally | WHO, 2017 | Economic burden justifies prevention investment |
| 50% reduction target by 2022 | WHO Challenge | Pharmacy is key intervention point |
| Community pharmacy: 0.01–5% error rate per item | James 2009 | Even "low" rates = thousands of errors nationally |

### 4.1.2 Root cause analysis (RCA) — 5 Whys technique

**Example near-miss:** Technician nearly dispensed **hydroxyzine** instead of **hydralazine**.

| Why # | Answer |
|---|---|
| 1 | Wrong drug selected from shelf |
| 2 | Packages look similar, stored adjacent |
| 3 | No separate storage policy for LASA drugs |
| 4 | Staff not trained on LASA list |
| 5 | No system for LASA identification at procurement |

**Corrective actions:** Separate storage, tall-man lettering labels, barcode verification, LASA training.

## Section 4.2 — High-alert medications (ISMP)

### ISMP International High-Alert Medications List (community pharmacy relevant):

| Category | Examples | Mandatory safeguards |
|---|---|---|
| Anticoagulants | Warfarin, enoxaparin, rivaroxaban | INR monitoring counseling; bleeding signs |
| Insulin | All types | Independent double-check; unit/mL verification |
| Opioids | Morphine, fentanyl, tramadol | Controlled register; dose verification |
| Chemotherapy (oral) | Capecitabine, temozolomide | Double-check; pregnancy screening |
| Immunosuppressants | Tacrolimus, cyclosporine | Brand consistency; level monitoring |
| Concentrated electrolytes | KCl concentrate (hospital) | Dilution verification |
| Methotrexate | Weekly dosing for RA | **Dose frequency check** — daily dosing = fatal error |
| Pediatric liquids | All | Weight-based dose double-check |

**Methotrexate error data:** ISMP has documented **multiple deaths** from methotrexate prescribed daily instead of weekly. Mandatory: verify dosing frequency on EVERY methotrexate prescription.

## Section 4.3 — LASA drugs — complete reference table

| Drug 1 | Drug 2 | Error consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Chlorpromazine** | **Chlorpropamide** | Severe hypoglycemia vs sedation | Tall-man: chlorproMAZINE vs chlorproPAMIDE |
| **Hydroxyzine** | **Hydralazine** | Wrong indication entirely | Separate shelves |
| **Prednisolone** | **Prednisone** | Usually interchangeable but different potency | Confirm with prescriber if critical |
| **Amoxicillin** | **Amoxapine** | Antibiotic vs antidepressant | Completely different areas |
| **Celebrex** | **Celexa** | NSAID vs antidepressant | Sound-alike alert |
| **Zyrtec** | **Zyprexa** | Antihistamine vs antipsychotic | Fatal if wrong |
| **Metformin** | **Metronidazole** | Different classes | Similar packaging risk |
| **Vincristine** (IV only) | **Vinblastine** | Fatal if wrong IV drug | Hospital only |

**Tall-man lettering (ISMP standard):** Use capital letters to distinguish syllables: *hydrOXYzine* vs *hydrALAzine*

## Section 4.4 — The 6 Rights of dispensing

| Right | Scientific basis | Verification method |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Patient | Identity errors → wrong patient harm | Name + DOB confirmation |
| 2. Drug | LASA, wrong selection | Barcode scan + visual check |
| 3. Dose | Calculation errors, decimal errors | Independent recalculation |
| 4. Route | Oral vs topical → toxicity or no effect | Label + counseling |
| 5. Time | Frequency errors → sub/supratherapeutic | Plain language instructions |
| 6. Documentation | Legal record, continuity of care | Pharmacy management system entry |

**6th Right (added by ASHP, 2010):** Right documentation — every dispensing action recorded with pharmacist identification.

## Section 4.5 — Barcode verification evidence

**Poon et al. (2006, *BMJ*):** Barcode-assisted dispensing reduced dispensing errors by **41.4%** and potential adverse drug events by **55.4%** in hospital pharmacy.

**Limitations:** Only effective if barcode database is current; does not catch **prescribing errors** (wrong drug prescribed correctly dispensed).

## Section 4.6 — Quality metrics

### Pharmacy quality indicators (adapted from FIP):

| Indicator | Formula | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Dispensing error rate | (Errors / items dispensed) × 100 | <0.1% |
| Near-miss reporting rate | Near-misses reported / month | Increasing (culture of reporting) |
| Counseling rate | (Rx counseled / Rx dispensed) × 100 | >90% |
| Waiting time | Minutes from Rx receipt to dispensing | <15 min (community) |
| Patient satisfaction | Survey score / 5 | >4.0 |

## Case study 4.1 — Fatal error analysis

**Thalidomide (historical, 1957–1961):**
- Prescribed for morning sickness in pregnancy
- Caused **phocomelia** (limb defects) in >10,000 children worldwide
- Led to creation of modern drug safety regulations (FDA 1962 Kefauver-Harris Amendment)
- **Lesson:** Pharmacist must verify drug safety in pregnancy (FDA categories / current labeling)

**FDA Pregnancy Risk Categories (legacy, replaced by PLLR 2015):**
- Modern labeling: Pregnancy/Lactation sections with risk summary, clinical considerations, data
