## Section 7.1 — Stability science

### 7.1.1 Degradation mechanisms

| Mechanism | Affected drugs | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| **Hydrolysis** | Aspirin, penicillins, esters | Dry storage, sealed containers |
| **Oxidation** | Adrenaline, vitamin C, unsaturated fats | Amber containers, nitrogen flushing, antioxidants |
| **Photolysis** | Nifedipine, riboflavin, ciprofloxacin | Amber vials, protect from light |
| **Thermal** | Insulin, vaccines, suppositories | Controlled temperature 15–25°C |
| **Microbial** | Reconstituted antibiotics, eye drops (multi-dose) | Preservatives; discard after opening period |

### 7.1.2 Temperature definitions (USP <659>)

| Storage condition | Temperature range | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Freezer** | −25°C to −10°C | Some vaccines during transport |
| **Refrigerator** | **2°C to 8°C** | Insulin, vaccines, growth hormone, eye drops (some) |
| **Cold** | Not exceeding 8°C | Same as refrigerator |
| **Cool** | 8°C to 15°C | Some suppositories |
| **Controlled room temperature** | **20°C to 25°C** (excursions 15–30°C) | Most tablets, capsules |
| **Warm** | 30°C to 40°C | Not for storage — transport limit |
| **Excessive heat** | Above 40°C | Damages most pharmaceuticals |

**Egypt climate challenge:** Summer ambient temperatures often exceed 40°C. Air conditioning in pharmacy is **not optional** — it is a pharmaceutical quality requirement.

### 7.1.3 Insulin storage science

| State | Temperature | Duration | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unopened vial/pen | 2–8°C (refrigerate) | Until expiry | Manufacturer stability data |
| Opened vial | 2–25°C (room temp OK) | **28 days** (most brands) | Bacterial growth risk after 28 days |
| Opened pen | 2–25°C | **28–42 days** (brand-specific) | Check individual SPC |
| Frozen insulin | **NEVER freeze** | Destroys protein structure | Irreversible denaturation |
| In-use insulin in summer >30°C | Cool bag recommended | Degradation accelerates | A1c may rise if degraded |

**Counseling script:** "Keep unopened insulin in fridge. Once opened, keep at room temperature (not fridge) for up to 28 days. Never freeze. If it looks cloudy (except NPH/isophane which is naturally cloudy), discard."

## Section 7.2 — Cold chain management

### 7.2.1 WHO cold chain standards

**WHO Performance, Quality and Safety (PQS) standards:**
- Refrigerator must maintain **+2°C to +8°C** at all times
- Daily temperature monitoring with **min/max thermometer** (record twice daily)
- Alarm for out-of-range temperatures
- **2-hour rule:** Vaccines/insulin exposed >8°C or <2°C for >2 hours may need discard (check specific product SPC)

## Section 7.2.3 — Humidity effects

**USP <1079>:** Relative humidity should be **35–65%** for most pharmaceuticals.

| Drug/formulation | Humidity sensitivity | Storage |
|---|---|---|
| Effervescent tablets (aspirin, Alka-Seltzer) | High — CO2 release | Tightly sealed, desiccant |
| Hygroscopic drugs (sodium valproate, ranitidine) | Absorbs moisture → clumping | Desiccant in container |
| Hard gelatin capsules | Shell becomes brittle (low RH) or soft/sticky (high RH) | 35–65% RH |
| Dry powder inhalers | Moisture affects dispersion | Keep dry, do not store in bathroom |

## Section 7.3 — Beyond-use dating

### 7.3.1 Reconstituted products

| Product | After reconstitution | Storage | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amoxicillin suspension | 7–14 days (check label) | Refrigerate 2–8°C | Manufacturer SPC |
| Cefixime suspension | 14 days | Room temp or refrigerate | Manufacturer SPC |
| Reconstituted IV antibiotics (hospital) | 24 hours refrigerated | Hospital pharmacy USP <797> | |
| Multi-dose eye drops (with preservative) | 28 days after opening | Room temp | Manufacturer |
| Multi-dose eye drops (unit-dose) | Single use — discard after | | |

**USP <795> (pharmacy compounding):** Beyond-use dates based on stability data, storage conditions, and container type.

## Section 7.4 — Expired medication science

### 7.4.1 SLEP (Shelf Life Extension Program) data

**FDA SLEP findings (published studies):**
- 88% of 122 drugs tested were stable ≥1 year past expiry (military stockpile)
- **BUT:** This applies to unopened, ideal storage conditions
- **Tetracycline exception:** Degraded tetracycline can cause **Fanconi syndrome** — never use expired
- **Liquid formulations** degrade faster than solids
- **Community pharmacy rule:** **Never dispense expired medication** — zero tolerance

### 7.4.2 Financial impact of expiry waste

| Strategy | Waste reduction |
|---|---|
| FIFO/FEFO strict implementation | 30–50% reduction |
| Order smaller quantities more frequently | 20–30% reduction |
| Monitor slow-moving stock monthly | 15–25% reduction |
| Return-to-supplier programs (short-dated) | Variable |
