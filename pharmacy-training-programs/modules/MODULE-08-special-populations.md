## Learning objectives

1. Calculate **weight-based pediatric doses** (mg/kg) with safety ceiling verification
2. Apply **Beers Criteria 2023** for potentially inappropriate medications (PIMs) in elderly

4. Counsel on **pregnancy risk** using FDA Pregnancy and Lactation Labeling Rule (PLLR)
5. Identify **geriatric pharmacokinetic changes** affecting drug handling

## Section 8.1 — Pediatric pharmacotherapy

### 8.1.1 Developmental pharmacokinetic differences

| Parameter | Neonate/infant | Child | Adult |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gastric pH | Higher (less acidic) → altered absorption | Approaching adult | Reference |
| Body water | 75–80% (more ECF) | Higher Vd for water-soluble drugs | 60% |
| Liver enzymes | Reduced CYP activity → slower metabolism | Adult levels by ~10 years | Full |
| Renal function | Immature GFR → reduced excretion | Adult GFR by ~1 year | Reference |
| Protein binding | Lower albumin → more free drug | Approaching adult | Reference |
| BBB permeability | Higher → CNS toxicity risk | Decreasing | Reference |

### 8.1.2 Pediatric dose calculation

**Formula:**
> Dose (mg) = Weight (kg) × Dose (mg/kg)

**Safety rule:** Always verify calculated dose against **adult maximum dose** — never exceed adult dose even if mg/kg calculation is higher.

**Worked examples:**

**Example 1 — Paracetamol:**
> Child: 12 kg, age 2 years. Dose: 15 mg/kg/dose QID PRN.
> Calculation: 12 × 15 = **180 mg per dose**
> Formulation: Paracetamol 120mg/5mL → 180/120 × 5 = **7.5 mL per dose**
> Max daily: 60 mg/kg/day = 12 × 60 = 720mg/day. At QID: 180 × 4 = 720mg ✓ (at ceiling)
> **NEVER use 500mg tablets in this child**

**Example 2 — Amoxicillin (otitis media):**
> Child: 18 kg. Dose: 40 mg/kg/day divided TDS.
> Daily dose: 18 × 40 = 720 mg/day
> Per dose: 720 ÷ 3 = **240 mg TDS**
> Suspension 250mg/5mL: 240/250 × 5 = **4.8 mL TDS**

### 8.1.3 Pediatric high-risk drugs

| Drug | Risk in children | Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| Aspirin (<16 years) | **Reye's syndrome** risk with viral illness | Paracetamol, ibuprofen (>3 months) |
| Tetracyclines (<8 years) | Permanent tooth discoloration, enamel hypoplasia | Amoxicillin, azithromycin |
| Fluoroquinolones | Cartilage damage in growing animals (theoretical human risk) | Reserve for resistant infections |
| Codeine (<12 years) | Respiratory depression (CYP2D6 ultra-rapid metabolizers) | Paracetamol, ibuprofen |
| Chloramphenicol (neonates) | Gray baby syndrome (immature glucuronidation) | Avoid in neonates |
| Benzocaine teething gels | Methemoglobinemia | Teething ring, paracetamol |

## Section 8.2 — Geriatric pharmacotherapy

### 8.2.1 Aging pharmacokinetic changes

| Change | Effect on drugs | Clinical example |
|---|---|---|
| ↓ Gastric emptying | Delayed absorption | Slower onset |
| ↓ Lean body mass, ↑ fat | ↑ Vd for lipophilic drugs | Diazepam accumulates |
| ↓ Albumin | ↑ Free fraction of protein-bound drugs | Phenytoin toxicity at "normal" total levels |
| ↓ Liver mass and CYP activity | ↓ Clearance | Reduced doses needed |
| ↓ GFR (avg loss 1 mL/min/year after age 40) | ↓ Renal excretion | Metformin, digoxin, gabapentin dose adjust |
| ↓ BBB integrity | ↑ CNS sensitivity | Benzodiazepines → falls, confusion |

**Beers 2023 — Key PIMs in community pharmacy:**

| Drug/class | Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Benzodiazepines (long-acting) | Falls, cognitive impairment, delirium | Avoid; max 2 weeks if necessary |
| Anticholinergics (diphenhydramine, oxybutynin) | Dementia risk, constipation, urinary retention | Avoid in elderly |
| NSAIDs (chronic use) | GI bleeding, AKI, heart failure exacerbation | Avoid if eGFR <30; use paracetamol |
| Proton pump inhibitors (>8 weeks) | C. difficile, fracture, Mg deficiency | Reassess need; lowest dose |
| Sulfonylureas (glibenclamide) | Severe prolonged hypoglycemia | Prefer gliclazide or DPP-4i |
| Digoxin >0.125mg/day | Toxicity in reduced renal function | Max 0.125mg in elderly |
| Sliding scale insulin alone | Hypoglycemia without benefit | Use basal-bolus regimen |
| Tramadol | Falls, serotonin syndrome, seizures | Avoid if possible |

### 8.2.2 Renal dose adjustment

**eGFR calculation (CKD-EPI 2021 equation):**
> Use online calculator or lab-reported eGFR

**Dose adjustment table:**

| eGFR (mL/min) | CKD stage | Metformin | Gabapentin | Digoxin | NSAIDs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ≥60 | 1–2 | Full dose | Full dose | Full dose | Caution |
| 30–59 | 3a–3b | Max 1000mg/day | Reduce 50–75% | Reduce dose | Avoid chronic |
| 15–29 | 4 | **Contraindicated** | Reduce 75% | Reduce 50% | Contraindicated |
| <15 | 5 | Contraindicated | Reduce per label | Avoid | Contraindicated |

## Section 8.3 — Pregnancy and lactation

### 8.3.1 FDA Pregnancy Labeling (PLLR 2015)

Replaced old A/B/C/D/X categories with narrative sections:
- **Pregnancy exposure registry** information
- **Risk summary** (human + animal data)
- **Clinical considerations** (disease effect, dose adjustments during pregnancy)
- **Data** section

**Key safe/unsafe drugs in pregnancy:**

| Safe (generally) | Avoid/contraindicated |
|---|---|
| Paracetamol | ACE inhibitors (2nd/3rd trimester) — fetal renal damage |
| Most penicillins, cephalosporins | Warfarin (1st trimester — teratogenic) |
| Insulin | Statins (teratogenic) |
| Levothyroxine (dose may ↑) | Methotrexate (abortifacient, teratogenic) |
| Folic acid (essential) | Valproate (neural tube defects, cognitive impairment) |
| | Tetracyclines (bone/teeth) |
| | Fluoroquinolones (cartilage) |
| | Live vaccines |

**Thalidomide legacy:** Led to modern teratogenicity testing requirements. **Isotretinoin (Accutane):** iPLEDGE program — pregnancy prevention mandatory.

### 8.3.2 Lactation compatibility (LactMed database principles)

| Drug | Breast milk transfer | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Paracetamol | Low | Compatible |
| Ibuprofen | Very low | Compatible |
| Amoxicillin | Low | Compatible |
| Codeine | Variable (CYP2D6 UM → morphine in milk) | Avoid — use paracetamol |
| Metformin | Low | Compatible |
| Carbamazepine | Moderate | Monitor infant |
| Lithium | High | Contraindicated or bottle-feed |

## Module 8 — Calculation exam

1. Child 8 kg, paracetamol 15 mg/kg/dose. Calculate mL of 120mg/5mL suspension per dose.
2. Child 25 kg, amoxicillin 45 mg/kg/day TDS. Calculate per dose in mg and mL (250mg/5mL).
3. Elderly patient eGFR 25, on metformin 1000mg BD. Is this appropriate? Recommend change.
4. Pregnant patient (8 weeks) prescribed lisinopril for hypertension. Action?
5. Breastfeeding mother prescribed codeine for post-Caesarean pain. Action?

### Answers
1. 8×15=120mg; 120/120×5 = 5 mL
2. 25×45=1125mg/day; 1125/3=375mg per dose; 375/250×5=7.5 mL
3. eGFR 25 — metformin contraindicated (<30). Contact physician for alternative (insulin, glibenclamide with caution)
4. ACE inhibitor contraindicated in pregnancy — contact physician immediately for methyldopa, labetalol, or nifedipine
5. Avoid codeine — CYP2D6 UM risk to infant. Recommend paracetamol, ibuprofen (if not contraindicated)
