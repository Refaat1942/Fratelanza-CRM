## Section 9.1 — Hand hygiene science

### 9.1.1 WHO 5 Moments

| Moment | Pharmacy scenario |
|---|---|
| 1. Before touching a patient/customer | Before counseling at counter |
| 2. Before clean/aseptic procedure | Before counting tablets, reconstituting |
| 3. After body fluid exposure risk | After handling soiled items |
| 4. After touching a patient | After helping with injection demo |
| 5. After touching patient surroundings | After handling prescriptions, money |

**Evidence:** Hand hygiene alone reduces healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) by **up to 50%** (WHO, 2009). Alcohol-based hand rub (ABHR) with 60–80% ethanol is **more effective** than soap for most pathogens, except *C. difficile* spores and norovirus.

### 9.1.2 Hand washing technique (WHO 6 steps)

1. Palm to palm
2. Right palm over left dorsum (and reverse)
3. Palm to palm fingers interlaced
4. Backs of fingers to opposing palms
5. Rotational rubbing of thumbs
6. Rotational rubbing of fingertips in palms

**Duration:** Minimum **40–60 seconds** for soap wash; **20–30 seconds** for ABHR.

## Section 9.2 — Contamination in pharmacy

### 9.2.1 Routes of contamination

| Route | Source | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Direct contact | Hands, equipment | Hand hygiene, glove use |
| Indirect contact | Contaminated surfaces, tools | Surface disinfection, dedicated tools |
| Airborne | Coughing, aerosolized products | Ventilation, mask if symptomatic |
| Vector | Insects, rodents | Pest control, sealed storage |

### 9.2.2 Never return rule

**USP and pharmacy law principle:** Once a medication has left the pharmacist's control and been dispensed to a patient, it **must never** be returned to stock.

**Scientific rationale:**
- Cannot verify storage conditions after leaving pharmacy
- Potential tampering or contamination
- Cross-contamination risk to other patients
- Legal liability

**Exception (some jurisdictions):** Unopened, sealed manufacturer packaging with temperature verification — check Egyptian regulations.

## Section 9.3 — COVID-19 and respiratory infection lessons

**WHO IPC guidelines (applicable permanently):**
- Physical barriers (sneeze guards at counter) reduce droplet transmission
- Surface disinfection: 70% ethanol or 0.1% sodium hypochlorite effective against enveloped viruses
- Symptomatic staff should not work in patient-facing roles
- Ventilation: minimum 6 air changes per hour in healthcare settings

## Section 9.4 — Waste management

| Waste type | Examples | Disposal |
|---|---|---|
| General pharmaceutical | Expired tablets (non-hazardous) | Authorized pharmaceutical waste collector |
| Hazardous/cytotoxic | Oral chemotherapy residues | Specialized hazardous waste |
| Sharps | Needles, lancets (if pharmacy offers injection) | Approved sharps container → incineration |
| Controlled drugs | Expired morphine, tramadol | Witnessed destruction per EDA regulations |
| Personal protective equipment | Used gloves, masks | Clinical waste stream |
