# Master Curriculum — 120-Hour Comprehensive Pharmacy Training Program
# المنهج الرئيسي — برنامج تدريب الصيدلة الشامل 120 ساعة

---

## Program overview / نظرة عامة

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Program name** | Comprehensive Evidence-Based Pharmacy Team Training |
| **Total hours** | 120 contact hours + 40 self-study hours |
| **Duration** | 8–12 weeks (3 sessions/week × 3–4 hours) |
| **Delivery** | Classroom + workshop + case studies + assessments |
| **Language** | Arabic (primary), English (scientific terms) |
| **Pass criteria** | ≥70% on each module assessment |
| **Accreditation note** | Internal CPD program — align with Egyptian Pharmacists Syndicate CPD requirements where applicable |

---

## Scientific framework / الإطار العلمي

This program is built on:

1. **WHO Patient Safety — Medication Without Harm** (Global Patient Safety Challenge, 2017–2024)
2. **Pharmaceutical Care model** (Hepler & Strand, 1990; updated FIP standards)
3. **Evidence-Based Medicine** principles (Sackett et al.)
4. **Egyptian Drug Authority (EDA)** regulations and pharmacy practice law
5. **International pharmacopeial standards** (USP storage, stability testing guidelines)
6. **Clinical pharmacology** references (BNF, Micromedex interaction principles, Goodman & Gilman core concepts)

## Weekly schedule (12-week model) / الجدول الأسبوعي

### Week 1–2: Module 1 — Foundations (12 hours)

| Session | Topic | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | History of pharmacy, healthcare system role, professional ethics | 3 |
| 1.2 | Pharmacy team structure, scope of practice, communication | 3 |
| 1.3 | Evidence-based practice introduction, literature literacy | 3 |
| 1.4 | Case workshop + Module 1 assessment | 3 |

**Learning outcomes:** Describe pharmacy's role in healthcare; apply ethical frameworks; identify team responsibilities.

---

### Week 3–4: Module 2 — Pharmacology & Pharmaceutics (16 hours)

| Session | Topic | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | Pharmacokinetics: absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion (ADME) | 4 |
| 2.2 | Pharmacodynamics: receptors, dose-response, therapeutic index | 4 |
| 2.3 | Dosage forms: tablets, capsules, liquids, injections — biopharmaceutics | 4 |
| 2.4 | Bioavailability, generic substitution science + assessment | 4 |

**Learning outcomes:** Explain ADME for common drug classes; interpret dose-response curves; justify generic vs brand selection scientifically.

---

### Week 5–6: Module 3 — Prescription Science (12 hours)

| Session | Topic | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Prescription anatomy, validity, legal requirements (Egypt) | 3 |
| 3.2 | Latin abbreviations — scientific basis and error prevention | 3 |
| 3.3 | Clinical checking: dose rationality, duration, duplicate therapy | 3 |
| 3.4 | Prescription error case studies + assessment | 3 |

**Learning outcomes:** Perform systematic prescription review; identify clinically significant errors; document interventions.

---

### Week 7–8: Module 4 — Dispensing & Patient Safety (14 hours)

| Session | Topic | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | Medication error epidemiology (WHO data); human factors science | 3.5 |
| 4.2 | The 5 Rights + 6th Right (right documentation); barcode evidence | 3.5 |
| 4.3 | High-alert medications; LASA (look-alike/sound-alike) drugs | 3.5 |
| 4.4 | Dispensing workflow lab + near-miss reporting + assessment | 3.5 |

**Learning outcomes:** Apply error-prevention strategies; manage high-alert medications; implement near-miss reporting.

---

### Week 9–10: Module 5 — Drug Interactions (14 hours)

| Session | Topic | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 5.1 | Interaction mechanisms: PK (CYP450, P-gp) and PD | 3.5 |
| 5.2 | Major interaction tables: anticoagulants, antibiotics, CNS drugs | 3.5 |
| 5.3 | Drug-food, drug-disease interactions with pathophysiology | 3.5 |
| 5.4 | Interaction case workshop + assessment | 3.5 |

**Learning outcomes:** Classify interaction mechanisms; predict clinically significant interactions; intervene appropriately.

---

### Week 11: Modules 6–7 (22 hours over 2 weeks compressed)

| Module | Key focus | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 6 — Counseling | Medication adherence science (WHO 50% non-adherence); teach-back method; health literacy | 12 |
| 7 — Storage | Stability testing (ICH guidelines); cold chain 2–8°C; humidity effects on APIs | 10 |

---

### Week 12: Modules 8–10 (30 hours — intensive week or extend to week 13–14)

| Module | Key focus | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 8 — Special populations | Pediatric weight-based dosing (mg/kg); geriatric Beers Criteria; pregnancy FDA categories | 14 |
| 9 — Infection control | WHO 5 Moments for Hand Hygiene; contamination routes in pharmacy | 8 |
| 10 — Law & ethics | Egyptian pharmacy law; controlled substance schedules; counterfeit detection | 8 |

## Assessment structure / هيكل التقييم

| Component | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Module MCQ tests (10 × 20 questions) | 50% | Knowledge recall and application |
| Case study write-ups (5 submitted) | 25% | Clinical reasoning documentation |
| Calculation exam | 15% | Dosing, dilution, infusion rates |
| Practical OSCE (dispensing simulation) | 10% | Observed dispensing + counseling |

**Final grade:** Weighted average ≥70% for certificate.

---

## Trainer qualifications / مؤهلات المدرب

Minimum requirements for program trainer:

- Licensed pharmacist (صيدلي مسجل) with ≥3 years community or hospital practice
- OR Pharm.D / clinical pharmacy qualification
- Familiarity with module content and assessment rubrics
- Completion of train-the-trainer session on case study facilitation

## Continuous improvement / التحسين المستمر

After each program cycle, collect:

- Trainee feedback scores (1–5 per module)
- Assessment pass rates by module
- Near-miss/error reports from pharmacy practice
- Trainer notes on outdated content

Review and update modules **annually** or when EDA issues major regulatory changes.
